## CSCI 4930/5930: Human-Computer Interaction
**Saint Louis University, Spring 2025**

Two invited guest lectures delivered to a combined undergraduate/graduate audience in the Human-Computer Interaction course.

---

### Lecture 1: UAS Simulation Fidelity: Bridging the Gap Between Virtual and Real

A graduate-level seminar covering the state of the art in high-fidelity simulation for small Uncrewed Aerial Systems (sUAS). Topics included:

- The *sim-to-reality gap*: why simulation environments fail to capture real-world flight conditions and what this means for autonomous system safety
- Technical deep-dive into DroneReqValidator (DRV), DroneWiS (CFD-based wind simulation), and React-G (GNSS multipath modeling)
- How game engine technology (Unreal Engine 5) is being repurposed as a rigorous mathematical framework for robotics validation
- Current open research problems in sUAS simulation fidelity
- Overview of the UAVLab-SLU research group and opportunities to join

---

### Lecture 2: Simulation Tools and Human-in-the-Loop Testing

A graduate-level lecture exploring the intersection of simulation infrastructure and human involvement in the testing cycle. Topics included:

- The role of human judgment in automated sUAS acceptance testing
- How DRV's interface design supports human-in-the-loop test configuration and result interpretation
- Cognitive and usability considerations when designing developer-facing simulation tools
- Human oversight in LLM-agent-driven testing pipelines (AutoSimTest)
- Discussion: where should automation end and human judgment begin in safety-critical systems?
