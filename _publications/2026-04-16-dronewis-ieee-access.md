This journal paper extends the DroneWiS framework with a comprehensive treatment of simulation-based testing for small Unmanned Aerial Systems (sUAS) in realistic windy conditions. The work addresses a critical gap in sUAS validation: existing simulation environments employ oversimplified wind models that fail to capture the complex aerodynamic interactions between wind, terrain, and urban structures.

**Key contributions:**
- Full CFD pipeline leveraging OpenFOAM for high-fidelity wind field generation in 3D digital twin environments
- Terrain voxelization algorithm for automated mesh generation from Unreal Engine scenes
- Comprehensive evaluation across diverse wind conditions (uniform, turbulent, multi-source) and environments
- Statistical analysis demonstrating improved simulation fidelity compared to state-of-the-art baselines
- Extended case studies validating wind model accuracy against real-world sUAS flight data

**Authors:** Bohan Zhang, Anjali Putta, Ankit Agrawal

**Venue:** IEEE Access (Early Access), April 2026.
