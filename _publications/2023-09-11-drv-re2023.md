Flight-time failures of small Uncrewed Aerial Systems (sUAS) can have a severe impact on people or the environment. We address the challenge of requirements-driven testing by presenting **DroneReqValidator (DRV)**, a platform that allows sUAS developers to define the operating context, configure multi-sUAS mission requirements, specify safety properties, and deploy their own custom sUAS applications in a high-fidelity 3D environment.

The DRV Monitoring system collects runtime data from sUAS and the environment, analyzes compliance with safety properties, captures violations, and produces detailed acceptance test reports for debugging and analysis. We report on two case studies validating sUAS mission behavior in various environmental contexts and a study with developers evaluating DRV's ability for designing requirements-based tests.

**Authors:** Ankit Agrawal, Bohan Zhang, Yashaswini Shivalingaiah, Michael Vierhauser, Jane Cleland-Huang

**Venue:** IEEE 31st International Requirements Engineering Conference (RE 2023)
