Traditional sUAS simulation tools such as Gazebo and AirSim use oversimplified wind models that ignore complex interactions with terrain and buildings. **DroneWiS** (Drone Wind Simulation) introduces DRV 2.0 — a novel component that automatically simulates realistic windy conditions to test the resilience of sUAS against wind.

DroneWiS leverages **Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD)** with OpenFOAM and a terrain scanning algorithm in Unreal Engine to compute unique wind flows caused by the interaction of wind with objects such as buildings and uneven terrain. This generates high-fidelity, dynamic wind vector data for uniform, turbulent, and multi-source wind types.

**Key contributions:**
- Terrain Scanning Algorithm that voxelizes the 3D digital twin environment for CFD input
- Automated OpenFOAM pipeline that configures and executes wind simulations
- Integration with DRV for seamless sUAS wind stress-testing
- Evaluated against real-world sUAS deployments demonstrating improved simulation fidelity

**Authors:** Bohan Zhang, Ankit Agrawal

**Venue:** 39th IEEE/ACM International Conference on Automated Software Engineering (ASE '24), Sacramento, CA, USA, October 27 – November 1, 2024.
