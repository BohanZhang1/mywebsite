GNSS multipath errors pose significant challenges for sUAS operating in reflective urban environments (e.g., buildings with glass facades), causing Non-Line-of-Sight (NLOS) signal reception that leads to erroneous position fixes. **React-G** (Reflective Environment Augmented Coordinates Tracking of GNSS) is a flexible simulation framework that approximates multipath signal path behaviors and characterizes reflection-induced deviations across a range of GNSS simulation tasks.

React-G integrates **Unreal Engine**, **Google Maps 3D Tiles**, and **Cesium for Unreal** to construct digital shadow representations of real-world urban environments. It uses a receiver-centric ray casting technique inspired by RF propagation principles to simulate NLOS multipath reflection effects, accounting for surface material reflectivity.

**Key contributions:**
- Novel ray casting methodology for GNSS LOS/NLOS multipath path approximation using game engine technology
- Integration of geospatial data (Google 3D Tiles, Celestrak satellite positions) for real-world-anchored simulations
- Comparative validation against NASA's NavQ real-world GNSS measurement system
- Configurable parameters: ray density, number of reflections, reflection angle threshold, GNSS receiver placement

Beyond sUAS navigation, React-G may also support autonomous ground vehicle localization and urban infrastructure design analysis.
