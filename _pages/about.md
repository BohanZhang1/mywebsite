I am a PhD Candidate in Computer Science at [Saint Louis University](https://www.slu.edu), where my research is fully sponsored by the [NASA University Leadership Initiative (ULI)](https://www.nasa.gov/directorates/armd/tacp/ui/uli/). My work focuses on improving the safety and reliability of small Uncrewed Aerial Systems (sUAS) through high-fidelity simulation, automated testing frameworks, and GNSS navigation modeling.

## Research

My research addresses the *sim-to-reality gap* — the critical challenge of ensuring that autonomous systems validated in virtual environments behave safely in the real world. I leverage game engine technology (Unreal Engine) to build rigorous, repeatable testing infrastructure for sUAS developers.

**Key projects and frameworks I have developed:**

- **[DroneReqValidator (DRV)](https://github.com/UAVLab-SLU/DRV_public)** — An automated sUAS testing ecosystem that generates high-fidelity 3D environments, monitors UAV safety parameters against predefined requirements, and produces detailed acceptance test reports. DRV has grown into a community-driven open-source project ([DroneWorld](https://github.com/oss-slu/DroneWorld)), actively maintained by undergraduate contributors through the OSS-SLU program at Saint Louis University.

- **DroneWiS** — A Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD)-based wind simulation module integrated into DRV. DroneWiS uses OpenFOAM and a terrain scanning algorithm to automatically compute realistic wind flows around buildings and complex terrain, enabling sUAS developers to stress-test their systems under physics-accurate wind conditions.

- **React-G** — A GNSS multipath simulation framework that uses Unreal Engine ray casting, Google Maps 3D Tiles, and Cesium for Unreal to model Non-Line-of-Sight (NLOS) signal reflections in dense urban environments, helping quantify navigation errors for sUAS.

- **AutoSimTest** — An LLM-agent-driven framework that automates the creation, execution, and analysis of sUAS simulation test scenarios, significantly reducing manual effort in the sUAS testing process.

## Background

I passed my PhD candidacy exam in 2025 and expect to complete my degree in May 2027. Prior to my doctoral studies, I earned my M.S. (GPA: 3.95/4.0) and B.S. in Computer Science from Saint Louis University, and worked as an Assistant Engineer at Beijing VRV Software Corp Ltd, where I contributed to large-scale enterprise software reliability and secure application design.

I also led an NSF-sponsored customer discovery initiative, conducting interviews with 22 robotics professionals to validate the commercial and research demand for high-fidelity sUAS simulation tools.
